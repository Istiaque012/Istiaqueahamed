## The core idea

One website for every aspect of one life. Not a CV. Not a clinic profile. A living document of who Istiaque Ahamed is, what he's building, and the life he's becoming.

## Positioning statement

> A Bangladeshi doctor shaped by his father's example, trained in public health in Australia, building healthcare systems and technology, telling stories through documentary, and writing honestly about the life in between.

The power is in the **combination**. Plenty of people are doctors. Plenty build apps. Very few are a doctor + public-health thinker + hospital director + tech founder + documentary-maker + writer, working across two countries. The site's whole job is to make that combination feel **intentional and coherent**, not scattered.

## What makes this rare (the edge)

| Most personal sites | This site |
|---|---|
| One identity (just doctor, or just founder) | Many identities, unified |
| Credentials-first | Story-first, credentials in support |
| Static and templated | Cinematic, animated, premium |
| Professional only | Professional + deeply personal (father, faith, fitness, writing) |

## The three emotional pillars

1. **Inheritance** — medicine came from his father. The [[Page - My Beloved Father]] section is the emotional root of everything.
2. **Building** — StudyRise, My Care Pro, hospital systems. He doesn't just practise; he builds. See [[Page - Projects]].
3. **Becoming** — the Australia journey, the discipline, the writing. A man in deliberate motion. See [[Page - Timeline]].

## What success looks like

- Someone searches "Istiaque Ahamed" → the site is the #1 result and tells the whole story.
- A stranger lands on the home page and within 30 seconds feels they understand who he is.
- Every audience (patient, colleague, collaborator, someone curious about him personally) finds something that resonates.
- The site feels unmistakably *him* — not a template anyone could own.

## Tone of voice

Confident but warm. Reflective but not soft. Disciplined. Honest. A little cinematic. Never corporate, never clinical. Reads like a thoughtful person talking to one other person.

## Primary audience

> "People who want to understand who I am, what I am building, and the life I am becoming."

This is deliberately broad because the site serves the goal of **recognition** — making a name. But broad doesn't mean unfocused. Below are the real audiences and what each one needs.

## Audience map

| Audience | What they want | Where the site serves them |
|---|---|---|
| People curious about me personally | The full human picture | [[Page - About]], [[Page - Timeline]], [[Page - My Beloved Father]], [[Page - Feed]] |
| Future patients | Trust, competence, warmth | [[Page - About]], [[Page - Work]] |
| Colleagues & healthcare pros | Credibility, ideas, systems thinking | [[Page - Blog]], [[Page - Work]], [[Page - Projects]] |
| Medical students | Honest perspective, guidance, StudyRise | [[Page - Blog]], [[Page - Projects]], [[Page - Journal]] |
| Future collaborators / investors | What he builds, how he thinks | [[Page - Projects]], [[Page - Course (AI in Healthcare)]] |
| People following the Australia journey | The arc, the discipline | [[Page - Timeline]], [[Page - Journal]] |
| Documentary / content audience | His films and stories | [[Page - Documentaries]] |

## Goals, in priority order

1. **Be recognised** — own "Istiaque Ahamed" across search and AI answers.
2. **Show the whole person** — every part of life in one place, coherently.
3. **Build an audience that follows the journey** — via the [[Page - Feed]] and consistent writing.
4. **Create a platform for what's next** — the course, future products, collaborations.

## What we are NOT optimising for (yet)

- Selling (the course stays "coming soon" — see [[Page - Course (AI in Healthcare)]])
- Newsletter list (leave room, don't build yet)
- Lead generation / clients

> [!note] Why broad-but-structured works here
> A personal brand site for recognition can serve many audiences *if* the navigation lets each one self-select quickly. The home page + feed give everyone an entry; the dedicated pages let each audience go deep. The risk to avoid is a homepage that tries to speak to everyone at once and ends up generic — see [[Page - Home]] for how we handle that.
